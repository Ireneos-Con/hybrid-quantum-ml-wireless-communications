Short note on the 3GPP channel model

The reference paper evaluates AE and QAE over Rayleigh, Rician, and 3GPP
block-fading channels. It uses the channel form y = hx + w, where h is the
complex channel coefficient, and assumes perfect CSI with equalization
x_hat = y / h.

For the 3GPP case, the paper only states that the 3GPP model is used with
mobile velocity 30 km/h. It references the MATLAB implementation of the 3GPP
Spatial Channel Model based on TR 25.996, but does not provide full
implementation details.

The missing details include the exact scenario, path-loss/shadowing handling,
normalization, and how the full MIMO/multipath SCM is reduced to the scalar h
used by the autoencoder.

Our first 3GPP implementation was simplified: h was generated from 6 paths and
20 subpaths with equal path power and random phases. This makes h close to a
complex Gaussian coefficient, so the result is naturally close to Rayleigh
fading.

We therefore implemented our own 3GPP TR 25.996-based effective block-fading
model. It still follows the paper-compatible form y = hx + w and x_hat = y / h,
but generates h using 6 paths, 20 subpaths per path, random path delays and
powers, TR 25.996 Table 5.2 AoA/AoD offsets, Doppler from 30 km/h mobility,
urban macro settings, and normalized average channel power.

This model should be described as our own 3GPP TR 25.996-based effective
block-fading model, not as an exact reproduction of the paper's hidden 3GPP
implementation.
